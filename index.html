BreachForums Analysis

:: Aggregate Security Metrics & Posture Analysis ::

[ Report & Findings ]

1. Executive Summary

In 2022/2023, BreachForums, a major cybercrime forum, experienced a significant data breach resulting in the public leak of its database. This event is noteworthy due to the forum's role in facilitating cybercriminal activities. Our analysis of the leaked data (databoose.sql), sourced from Resecurity, was conducted with an ethics-first approach, strictly avoiding PII handling.

2. Methodology

  • Isolation: Docker-based MySQL environment.
  • Sanitization: Schema cleaning & deduplication.
  • Aggregation: SQL counts only. No raw exports.

3. Comparison Findings

User Growth Trends

The registration data indicates significant growth spikes, particularly in April 2024 (24k+ users), suggesting viral marketing or migration events from other seized forums.

Security Posture

100% of users utilized Argon2i, indicating a mandatory high-security default in the MyBB configuration. This suggests the forum admins prioritized credential safety against scraping.

Demographics

Timezone data shows a massive concentration in UTC+0, likely a default setting, but tails exist in all major timezones. Language settings were predominantly English.

Anomaly Detection

  • Email: High usage of proton.me (29k) and gmail.com (240k).
  • Identity: Purely numeric usernames were rare (~1k). Short usernames (8 chars) were the mode.

4. Conclusion

The dataset reveals a "professionally" administered community with high-grade default security (Argon2i). The scale (323k users) confirms its status as a major hub before its disruption.
